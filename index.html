In June of 2017, my wife and I took our family on a grand road trip. We set out to enjoy a long weekend of camping and music at the 44th annual "Telluride Bluegrass Festival".

The Drive

We left St. Louis filled with excitement. As we drove, my wife and I tried to prepare our children for the mountain experience ahead. Sunscreen, lip-balm, and constant hydration are key for survival.

Camping

Because we had the coveted "Town Park" tickets, our camp site was located in the town of Telluride itself. We pitched our tent and made a camp on the shady bank of the San Miguel River. At an altitude of nearly 9,000 feet, the sun was intense during the day and the nights were quite cold.

The Festival

The town of Telluride is famous for its music and film festivals. The city's infrastructure and culture are built around decades of producing enormous, peaceful, and environmentally conscious festival for many thousands of people. One can really tell that the festival organizers have refined the art of throwing a party.

Exploring the town

With roots in the mining boom of the 19th century, Telluride has evolved to become a town focused on tourism and skiing. It's remote beauty and an "outlaw" reputation have made it a popular place for celebrities like Oprah Winfrey and Tom Cruise. The exclusive mountain communities that surround the small town are an enclave of the rich and famous.

The Takeaway

The majesty of the mountains, the joy of celebrating summer with family and friends, and the inspiring beauty of nature and music made a great family adventure.
